# Retained-Demand Audit Series for Institutionally Connected Digital Assets

This repository provides an AI-readable index for the **Retained-Demand Audit Series for Institutionally Connected Digital Assets**, an independent six-paper working series by **S. Meta**.

The complete PDF archive is maintained on OSF:

**OSF DOI:** https://doi.org/10.17605/OSF.IO/VQDUJ

## Purpose

This series develops a retained-demand audit framework for institutionally connected digital assets.

It does not present a price thesis, investment thesis, or asset-advocacy argument. XRP is used as a motivating and stress-test case in several papers, but the contribution is methodological and operational.

The core question is:

> Do institutionally connected digital assets create retained demand that must be carried by users, intermediaries, market makers, custodians, treasury operators, settlement platforms, or other infrastructure-layer actors?

## Series Structure

### Paper 1  
**From Usage to Retained Demand: A Structural Audit Framework for Institutionally Connected Digital Assets**

Core proposition:

> Usage is not retained demand.

Paper 1 distinguishes visible usage from retained demand and classifies retained demand into inventory demand, collateral demand, liquidity-buffer demand, and waiting liquidity.

---

### Paper 2  
**Expansion Is Not Closure: Settlement Stack Competition and the Conditional Relevance of External Connective Assets**

Core proposition:

> Expansion is not closure.

Paper 2 distinguishes infrastructure expansion from settlement-stack closure and explains why growing payment, stablecoin, tokenized-deposit, CBDC, or settlement infrastructure does not automatically establish the necessity of external connective assets.

---

### Paper 3  
**From Retained Demand to Required Liquidity Density: A Conditional Sizing Framework for Institutionally Connected Digital Assets**

Core proposition:

> Sizing is not a price thesis.

Paper 3 translates retained demand into required liquidity density while separating liquidity-feasibility analysis from price prediction.

---

### Paper 4  
**Compression, Bypass, and Amplification: An Applied Stack-Audit Framework for Institutionally Connected Digital Assets**

Core proposition:

> Infrastructure growth may compress, bypass, or amplify asset demand.

Paper 4 applies the framework at the settlement-stack level and distinguishes compression, bypass, and amplification as possible outcomes of infrastructure growth.

---

### Paper 5  
**Evidence Before Sizing: An Operational Audit Protocol for XRP-Adjacent Institutional Infrastructure**

Core proposition:

> Sizing is not rejected; sizing is gated.

Paper 5 introduces an evidence-gated audit protocol based on retained demand, asset selection, institutional friction, liquidity-density burden, and removal sensitivity.

---

### Paper 6  
**After User Abstraction: Backend Retained Demand and Just-in-Time Liquidity in Institutionally Connected Digital Assets**

Core proposition:

> Use without inventory is weak retained demand.

Paper 6 extends the framework from retained-demand type to retained-demand location after user abstraction. It distinguishes backend retained demand from backend capability, customer utility from asset necessity, and pre-positioned inventory from just-in-time liquidity.

## Methodological Distinctions

The series separates:

- usage from retained demand,
- expansion from closure,
- sizing from price prediction,
- infrastructure growth from asset-demand amplification,
- evidence from sizing eligibility,
- user abstraction from backend retained-demand formation,
- customer utility from asset necessity,
- just-in-time liquidity from pre-positioned inventory.
