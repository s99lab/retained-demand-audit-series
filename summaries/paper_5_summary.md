# Paper 5 Summary

## Title

**Evidence Before Sizing: An Operational Audit Protocol for XRP-Adjacent Institutional Infrastructure**

---

## Core Proposition

> Sizing is not rejected; sizing is gated.

Paper 5 turns the retained-demand framework developed in Papers 1–4 into an operational audit protocol.

It argues that XRP-adjacent institutional infrastructure should not be admitted into liquidity-density sizing merely because it is visible, strategically important, technically supported, or narratively close to XRP.

Sizing becomes appropriate only after evidence passes a defined sequence of audit gates.

---

## Role in the Series

Paper 5 is the operational control layer of the Retained-Demand Audit Series.

The earlier papers define the core analytical questions:

- Paper 1 distinguishes visible usage from retained demand.
- Paper 2 distinguishes infrastructure expansion from settlement-stack closure.
- Paper 3 separates conditional liquidity sizing from price narrative.
- Paper 4 classifies stack outcomes as compression, bypass, or amplification.

Paper 5 specifies the order in which those questions should be answered before any layer becomes eligible for filtered liquidity sizing.

In short:

> Papers 1–4 define what to ask.  
> Paper 5 defines when the evidence is strong enough to size.

---

## Five Eligibility Gates

Paper 5 introduces five eligibility gates.

A layer becomes stronger only as it passes through these gates in sequence.

1. **Retained Demand Gate**  
   Does XRP appear in held form, such as inventory, collateral, liquidity buffer, market-making inventory, settlement inventory, or waiting liquidity?

2. **Selection Gate**  
   Is XRP actually selected in the relevant workflow, rather than merely available, supported, or technically usable?

3. **Institutional Friction Gate**  
   Can XRP be practically retained, governed, accounted for, risk-managed, custodied, approved, and used within institutional constraints?

4. **Liquidity-Density Burden Gate**  
   Does the retained XRP demand create a meaningful burden on usable float, available depth, turnover, positioning, execution windows, or slippage tolerance?

5. **Removal-Sensitivity Gate**  
   Would removing XRP materially worsen cost, timing, slippage, liquidity access, collateral mobility, capital efficiency, stress-time execution, or operational resilience?

The fifth gate is the strongest gate.

The question is not whether XRP is present.

The question is whether XRP matters when removed.

---

## Evidence Gradient

Paper 5 also introduces an evidence gradient for classifying XRP-adjacent layers.

| Level | Evidence State | Sizing Implication |
|---|---|---|
| Level 0 | Narrative adjacency only | Not sizing-eligible |
| Level 1 | Infrastructure ready / selection pending | Monitoring only |
| Level 2 | Selection indicated / institutional admissibility unproven | Not yet sizing-eligible |
| Level 3 | Retained demand / institutional use evidence emerging | Potential sizing candidate |
| Level 4 | Liquidity-density burden visible | Sizing-relevant if specified |
| Level 5 | Removal-sensitive dependence established | Strongest sizing eligibility |

The gradient prevents weak evidence from being overread while still allowing partially meaningful evidence to be tracked.

## Key XRP-Adjacent Layers

The paper applies the framework to several XRP-adjacent institutional layers, including:

- Ripple Payments
- RLUSD
- XRPL base activity
- XRPL AMM / DEX liquidity
- Ripple Custody
- Ripple Prime / Hidden Road
- Ripple Treasury
- tokenization and RWA settlement
- collateral and margin infrastructure
- derivatives and hedging
- market-making inventory
- stress-time liquidity
- cross-ledger settlement
- agent commerce / machine payments

The paper does not treat these layers as XRP demand by default.

Each layer must be tested independently.

---

## Main Public-Evidence Conclusion

Paper 5 concludes that current public evidence supports strong XRP-adjacent domain expansion.

However, it does not establish stack-wide XRP-specific amplification.

The paper identifies narrower future evidence zones as especially important:

- market-making inventory
- collateral and margin infrastructure
- Ripple Prime / Hidden Road
- stress-time liquidity
- institutional XRPL liquidity
- cross-ledger settlement

These zones matter because they sit closer to retained demand, institutional admissibility, liquidity-density burden, and removal sensitivity than broad ecosystem visibility does.

---

## What This Paper Does Not Claim

Paper 5 does not claim that XRP is indispensable.

It does not claim that Ripple's institutional expansion automatically creates XRP demand.

It does not claim that RLUSD, XRPL activity, custody, treasury tooling, tokenization, or prime brokerage necessarily amplify XRP retained demand.

It does not provide a price forecast.

It does not provide an investment recommendation.

It does not reject sizing.

It gates sizing.

---

## Practical Use

Use Paper 5 when evaluating whether a specific XRP-adjacent development should be included in filtered liquidity sizing.

The correct question is not:

> Is this development bullish?

The correct question is:

> Which evidence gate, if any, has this development actually passed?
