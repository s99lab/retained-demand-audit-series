# Paper 5 Summary

## Title

**Evidence Before Sizing: An Operational Audit Protocol for XRP-Adjacent Institutional Infrastructure**

## Core Proposition

**Sizing is not rejected; sizing is gated.**

This paper turns the retained-demand framework developed in Papers 1–4 into an operational audit protocol.

It argues that liquidity sizing should not begin from speculative price targets, narrative conviction, or infrastructure adjacency. Instead, sizing should be considered only after evidence passes a sequence of audit gates.

## Role in the Series

Paper 5 is the operational protocol paper of the Retained-Demand Audit Series.

Papers 1–4 define the core questions:

- What is retained demand?
- Does expansion reach closure?
- What liquidity density would retained demand require?
- Does infrastructure compress, bypass, or amplify asset demand?

Paper 5 specifies the order in which these questions should be answered.

Its role is procedural rather than promotional.

Although this paper uses XRP-adjacent institutional infrastructure as a motivating case, the audit protocol is intended as a broader evidence-gating method for institutionally connected digital assets.

## Key Concepts

### Evidence Before Sizing

The paper does not reject sizing analysis.

Instead, it argues that sizing must be downstream of evidence.

Before estimating liquidity density or potential asset value, the analyst must first evaluate whether the asset is actually retained, selected, institutionally admissible, liquidity-burdened, and removal-sensitive.

### Five Audit Gates

The paper introduces five audit gates:

1. **Retained demand**  
   Does the asset need to be held, reserved, collateralized, or buffered?

2. **Asset selection**  
   Is the asset actually selected within the relevant institutional workflows?

3. **Institutional friction**  
   Can institutions practically hold, custody, account for, and risk-manage the asset?

4. **Liquidity-density burden**  
   Does the asset face a meaningful liquidity burden under operational constraints?

5. **Removal sensitivity**  
   Would removing the asset materially worsen cost, latency, settlement reliability, liquidity access, collateral efficiency, or routing outcomes?

### XRP-Adjacent Infrastructure

The paper uses XRP-adjacent infrastructure as a motivating case.

This includes areas such as payments, settlement, liquidity, XRPL, RLUSD, tokenization, RWA, custody, treasury, and institutional routing.

However, the paper does not treat adjacency as evidence of demand.

The term “XRP-adjacent” should be read as a stress-test context for the audit protocol, not as a limitation of the method to XRP alone.

### Removal Sensitivity

Removal sensitivity is a key audit criterion.

The strongest evidence for retained demand is not merely that an asset is present, but that removing it would create measurable degradation in system performance.

## What This Paper Does

This paper provides an operational sequence for evaluating whether XRP-adjacent or institutionally connected infrastructure has produced evidence strong enough to justify liquidity-density sizing.

It helps prevent premature valuation narratives.

It also provides a disciplined way to classify public information as:

- domain expansion
- possible asset selection
- retained-demand evidence
- institutional friction evidence
- liquidity-density evidence
- removal-sensitivity evidence

## What This Paper Does Not Claim

This paper does not claim that XRP, or any other asset, has already passed all audit gates.

It does not claim that infrastructure expansion alone proves retained demand.

It does not provide a price target.

It does not argue that sizing should never be done.

It does not claim that the audit protocol applies only to XRP.

Instead, it argues that sizing should only be performed after the relevant evidence gates are satisfied.

## How to Read It

Read Paper 5 as the series’ practical audit manual.

Its central question is:

**Has the evidence become strong enough to move from structural possibility to sizing eligibility?**

The answer must be gated, not assumed.

## Falsification / Audit Relevance

A retained-demand or sizing thesis weakens if:

- infrastructure expands while the asset remains optional
- workflows can operate without pre-positioned asset inventory
- stablecoins, fiat rails, tokenized deposits, or internal ledgers bypass the asset
- institutions do not hold or retain the asset
- liquidity burden is absorbed by substitutes
- removal of the asset does not degrade system outcomes
- evidence remains limited to narrative, branding, or adjacency

A thesis strengthens only when evidence moves across multiple gates.
